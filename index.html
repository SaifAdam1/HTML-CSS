Depression, An illness not understood by many

“Every man has his secret sorrows which the world knows not; and often times we call a man cold when he is only sad.” ― Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

People often associate depression with past trauma or some heinous event in one’s life that led them to become a victim of depression. But is that right? Is it really only scary
events that cause depression? And do people with depression all want to wear black all the time, never talk to anyone, and only think of ending their lives? These opinions are
nothing but general stereotypes that don't even represent 3% of cases of depression.

Depression doesn’t necessarily require a past trauma or event to affect someone. It varies significantly from person to person. Someone can suffer from depression for various
reasons, from a broken relationship to witnessing or experiencing a horrific event, such as what is currently happening to Palestinians.
